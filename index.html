The problem

Financial education
is broken for teens.

Only 3.5% of Indians invest in equities. Not because they can't — because nobody taught them how, early enough.

01
Passive learning
Reading about a P/E ratio is different from watching your portfolio drop because of one. No platform bridges that gap for Indian students.
02
No Indian context
Investopedia uses S&P 500 examples. Indian teenagers need Nifty, HDFC Bank, and Zerodha — not the NYSE. Context makes concepts stick.
03
Too late, too abstract
Most Indians first encounter investing in their 20s, after financial habits are already set. Starting at 16 with zero real risk changes everything.
